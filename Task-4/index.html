BMI Information:

This section provides detailed information about Body Mass Index (BMI), its calculation, categories, and health implications.

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure that helps assess whether a person has a healthy body weight relative to their height. It is widely used to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

How is BMI Calculated?

Metric Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²)

US Units Formula: BMI = (703 × weight (lbs)) / height² (in²)

Example: A 160 lb person with a height of 5'10" (70 in) → BMI = (703 × 160) / (70²) = 23.0

Health Risks of Being Overweight

  • High blood pressure
  • Unhealthy cholesterol levels
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Heart disease and stroke
  • Gallbladder disease
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Sleep apnea
  • Certain cancers (e.g. breast, colon)
  • Depression and anxiety
  • Reduced quality of life

Health Risks of Being Underweight

  • Malnutrition and anemia
  • Osteoporosis
  • Weakened immune system
  • Developmental issues in youth
  • Fertility problems in women
  • Increased surgical risk

Limitations of BMI

While useful, BMI does not account for muscle mass, bone density, and fat distribution. It may misclassify muscular individuals as overweight and underestimate fat in sedentary individuals.

  • Athletes may have high BMI due to muscle
  • Older adults tend to have more body fat
  • Women generally have higher fat content than men at the same BMI
  • Ethnic and age factors affect BMI accuracy

What is BMI Prime?

BMI Prime is the ratio of a person's BMI to the upper limit of the normal range (25 kg/m²).

Formula: BMI Prime = BMI / 25

What is the Ponderal Index?

Ponderal Index (PI) is similar to BMI but uses height cubed instead of squared. It is more accurate for very tall or short individuals.

US Formula: PI = height (in) / ∛mass (lbs)

Metric Formula: PI = mass (kg) / height³ (m³)

Example: A person who is 70 inches tall and weighs 160 lbs → PI = 70 / ∛160 = 12.9
